Yoghurt Cake

Wanna make a tasty cake? Here is a recipe to cook without a scale, a measure unit or whatever else, you just need a yogurt pot.
You can use a yoghurt of any flavour, chocolate, strawberry, banana…

Here is what you need :

  • 1 pot of yoghurt, nature or whatever flavour you like as far as there isn’t chunk (or piece) inside (like fruit chunks)
  • 1 pot and a half of white sugar
  • 3 pots of plain flour
  • 1/2 pot of milk
  • 1 sachet of baking powder
  • 1 sachet of vanilla sugar
  • 2 large eggs (or medium, depends on the size of the pot)

Optional

  • Chocolate powder
  • Chocolate chips
  • Cake icing
  • Marshmallow

This recipe is really simple, and pretty quick to make.

  1. Put the yoghurt in a mug in order to use it as measuring unit
  2. Mix eggs, white sugar and vanilla sugar
  3. Add the yoghurt and blend
  4. Add milk, flour and baking powder

From here, you have different options :

Option 1 :
Your dough is done, you can add chocolate chips in the dough or at the top of your cake and bake it.

Option 2 : ONLY FOR THOSE WHO USE TASTELESS YOGHURT
You can add chocolate powder or another chocolate powder and bake your dough, you will get a chocolate cake.

Option 3 :
You can divide you dough in 2 or 3, and add chocolate powder in one, chocolate chips in another one, marshmallow or whatever you like in parts to get a multi-layer cake.

BAKING

Bake your cake at 180°C for 30min, then lower your oven at 150°C and check every 5 minutes if you cake is baked putting a knife into the cake.

When I cook that cake, I usually finish it up wrapping it with chocolate icing, on which I add pieces of broken biscuits, fruits or sweet decorations.
